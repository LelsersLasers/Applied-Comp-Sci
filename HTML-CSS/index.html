Mansion

Again, who wouldn't want a bigger house? Large, spacoius, and usually with a ocean view and/or multiple pools. You spend most of your time in your house, either sleeping, homework (which I am definitely doing not playing video games, how dare you suggest that), cooking (read: setting toaster over on fire), or eating (mostly pasta, some pizza, lots of chocolate). A bigger house gives you more space, and often can me more relaxing as you don't feel like you are being smashed. Also, the extra space lets you have more things (because you have more space to store them). I don't care much for pools, but again, why not? As for ocean view, that would be quite nice. Imagine sitting on the 4th story deck of your house looking at the sun hovering over the ocean while you rip your hair out doing English homework. However, a large house has it's downsides. It's less 'cozy' feeling, and it takes a lot more to clean it/keep it organized. Also, my immediate family has only 4 people, so we don't really need the extra space, and imagine having to walk 4 flights of stairs to get the pen you forgot in your room.

As for costs, they can get a bit price-y. The one pictured above costs around $117,000,000 (117 million)

Giant gas rc car

It is a rc car. But gas powered. It uses a 2-stroke engine like a dirt bike or chainsaw (compared to an actual car which uses a 4-stroke engine). You can learn more about the differences here. However, being an rc car has it's benifits:
  1. Can drive it to it's limits, without the fear of crashing
  2. Better power to weight ratio than real cars
  3. Can drive it on dirt bike/pump tracks, jump it, race it (things you can't do with most normal cars)
  4. It's less expensive than a full sized car
It is still expensive though, around $1,500 dollars which is a lot for what is basically a very large toy. Fancy computer

A computer can be used for almost infinite things. Some examples of how it can be used:
  • Browse the web
  • Youtube
  • Video games
  • School work (which I am doing not playing video games)
  • Work
  • Writing
In addition, a more powerful computer makes using it more efficient.
  • More RAM = more google chrome tabs open at once
  • More storage = more space to store stuff (games, pictures, files, etc)
  • Faster disk = less time waiting for files to open
  • Better GPU = games run better (smoother with more detailed graphics)
  • Better CPU = just better in general
However, as computer parts/specs get better, they get physically bigger and they require more power (meaning they also require more cooling). The cooling takes space, and they computers themselves get very large, making them hard to move around. So if I wanted go take to the computer to my friends house to player video games with them, I could not.
