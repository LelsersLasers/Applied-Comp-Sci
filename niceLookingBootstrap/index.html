A Dog Was Crying Tonight in Wicklow Also 


In memory of Donatus Nwoga

When human beings found out about death
They sent the dog to Chukwu with a message:
They wanted to be let back to the house of life.
They didn’t want to end up lost forever
Like burnt wood disappearing into smoke
Or ashes that get blown away to nothing.
Instead, they saw their souls in a flock at twilight
Cawing and headed back for the same old roosts
And the same bright airs and wing-stretches each morning.
Death would be like a night spent in the wood:
At first light they’d be back in the house of life.
(The dog was meant to tell all this to Chukwu).

But death and human beings took second place
When he trotted off the path and started barking
At another dog in broad daylight just barking
Back at him from the far bank of a river.

And that is how the toad reached Chukwu first,
The toad who’d overheard in the beginning
What the dog was meant to tell. ‘Human beings,’ he said
(And here the toad was trusted absolutely),
‘Human beings want death to last forever.’

Then Chukwu saw the people’s souls in birds
Coming towards him like black spots off the sunset
To a place where there would be neither roosts nor trees
Nor any way back to the house of life.
And his mind reddened and darkened all at once
And nothing that the dog would tell him later
Could change that vision. Great chiefs and great loves
In obliterated light, the toad in the mud,
The dog crying out all night behind the corpse house.
Seamus Heaney

My Analysis

And why I thought it was about toads

I mean, how could it not be about toads? It literally says that toads caused this all. What else could 

    ‘Human beings,’ he said
    (And here the toad was trusted absolutely),
    ‘Human beings want death to last forever.’
mean? It clearly means that the toad caused this all, right? At least that is what I though it meant. I also failed to understand what "Chukwu" was.
" Chukwu is the supreme being of Igbo spirituality. In the Igbo pantheon, Chukwu is the source of all other Igbo deities and is responsible for assigning them their different tasks. The Igbo people believe that all things come from Chukwu, who brings the rain necessary for plants to grow and controls everything on Earth and the spiritual world. They believe Chukwu to be an undefinable omnipotent and omnipresent supreme deity that encompasses everything in space and space itself. "
Wikipedia: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chukwu
However because of my bad hand writing, I read Igbo as Igloo. This caused a lot of problems. The first one was that it made "Chukwu" the "supreme being of Igloo". Not only does that sentence not make any sense, it also influnced my understanding of the poem. Igloos are usally in very cold places, meaning that farming/agriculture is not an option. This to me meant that these people in the story were hunters (only way to get food). Thus the idea of hunting came about. So, with toads being the 'bad guys' and hunting also an 'imporant' meaning, it is clear that that the poem is about hunting toads.


What It Actually Meant

And why its not about toads

If you understand Chukwu, the poem gets a lot clearer and becomes less about toads. Chukwu, being the supreme god controls life and death. When the humans find out they could die permanently, they don't like it, and they want to not be dead (who can blame them?). So they send the dog to tell Chukwu that they want to not be permanently dead, but the dog gets distracted and instead of the dog telling Chukwu that the humans don't wnt to be dead, the toad tells him that they do want dead to last forever. The dog tried to convince Chukwu that the toad is lying, but he is unable to. The dog is then seen crying behind the corpse house, showing that he feels responsible. This related back to the title of them poem, "A Dog Was Crying Tonight in Wicklow Also". The author Seamus Heaney lived in Wicklow, and he is comparing himself to the dog. He feels responsible for the death of Donatus Nwoga, and that he was distracted and would have been able to prevent him from dying.
